The Water Cycle

Understanding how water moves through our environment

Evaporation Condensation Precipitation Collection

Step 1: Evaporation

Evaporation is the process where water turns from a liquid to a gas due to heat. The water in rivers, lakes, and oceans evaporates and rises into the air, forming water vapor.

Step 2: Condensation

Condensation occurs when water vapor in the air cools down and changes back into liquid form, creating clouds.

Step 3: Precipitation

When clouds get heavy, the water falls back to Earth as precipitation in the form of rain, snow, sleet, or hail.

Step 4: Collection

Water that falls to the ground is collected in oceans, lakes, and rivers, where it will eventually evaporate again, continuing the cycle.

Fun Fact!

Did you know that 97% of Earth's water is in the oceans?
